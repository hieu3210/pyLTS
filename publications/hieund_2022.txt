Abstract— Linguistic time series forecasting model (LTS-FM)
which is proposed by Hieu et al. by utilizing hedge algebras theory
is an efficient forecasting model. Instead of partitioning the
universe of discourse (UD) of the linguistic variable into subintervals and assigning fuzzy sets to them, it establishes a
formalism to convert historical numeric time series data into
linguistic one based on numerical semantics of words which are
transformed from the semantically quantifying mapping (SQM)
values of the corresponding words. Therefore, a LTS-FM is
established in such a way that it handles directly words of
linguistic variable and their qualitative semantics. However, the
fuzziness parameter values of the LTS-FM which determine the
SQM values of words are currently specified by human experts, so
the forecasted results may not be optimal. This paper proposes a
hybrid LTS-FM in which particle swarm optimization is utilized
to optimize the fuzziness parameter values. A new formula of
computing crisp forecasted values is also proposed. The
experimental studies carried out over two practical forecasting
problems of the enrollments of University of Alabama and killed
in car road accident in Belgium show that the proposed forecasting
model obtains better forecasted results.
Keywords—linguistic time series, LTS, hedge algebras, linguistic
logical relationship.

I. INTRODUCTION
Fuzzy time series forecasting model (FTS-FM) proposed by
Song and Chissom [1-3] and then enhanced by Chen [4], in
which fuzzy sets are considered the computational semantics of
words. In that forecasting model, the universe of discourse (UD)
of linguistic variable is divided into sub-intervals, fuzzy sets are
designed on them, and then linguistic words are assigned to the
designed fuzzy sets. The forecasting procedures are established
based on fuzzy logical relationships. The studies of them have
opened a new intensive research field which has been attracting
the research community publishing their researches to enhance
the models and solve practical problems. The methodological
studies in the literature include simplifying computational
method [4], sub-interval length optimization by applying
particle swarm optimization (PSO) [5, 16], genetic algorithm
(GA) [7], etc. or clustering techniques [26], high-order models
[7, 9], multi-factor models [20], application of efficient
defuzzification techniques [5, 16], linguistic time series [8, 9,
18, 23], etc. The application-oriented studies focus on the

Despite the considerable achievements, there are some
drawbacks of FTS-FMs which can briefly be described as
follows:
•

The word-semantic problem: Although the linguistic
words present in fuzzy time series with their inherent
semantics well-familiar with the human users, they are
only considered as linguistic labels intuitively assigned
to the designed fuzzy sets. There is no formal
connection between the inherent semantics of linguistic
labels with their associated fuzzy sets.

•

The determination of the designed fuzzy sets: It is
obvious that the inherent semantic of linguistic labels is
objective because all users understand the same its
meaning. Meanwhile, the designed fuzzy sets are
entirely subjective because they depend very much on
the designers.

With the drawbacks described above, the need of a formal
formalism that can directly handle linguistic words in solving a
given time series forecasting problem is arising.
Hedge algebras (HA) introduced by Nguyen et al. [10, 11]
model linguistic variables inherent semantic structures in an
axiomatic way. HA establishes formal formalism to handle
directly linguistic words and has been applied efficiently in the
fields of classification [12, 17], regression [13], fuzzy control
[15], time series forecasting [8, 9, 18, 23], image processing
[14], and so on. Applied to deal with time series forecasting
problems, HA establishes a formalism to convert historical
numeric time series into linguistic time series (LTS) based on
numerical semantics of words which are computed from the
semantically quantifying mapping (SQM) values of the
corresponding words. Then, linguistic logical relationships
(LLRs) are established from LTS and based on them linguistic
logical relationship groups (LLRGs) are established. They are
the basis for establishing forecasting rules. However, we can see
that although the LTS-FMs proposed in [8, 9, 18, 23] handle
directly words of linguistic variable, they are still restricted more
or less in the methodological frame of FTS-FMs. Besides, the

fuzziness parameter values of those LTS-FMs which determine
the SQM values of words are currently specified by human
experts. Therefore, the generated LTS may not be optimal
leading to the forecasting accuracies may not be good enough.
This paper proposes a method of optimizing the fuzziness
parameter values of LTS-FMs by utilizing particle swarm
optimization (PSO) [22] so as to improve their forecasting
accuracies. A new formula of computing crisp forecasted values
based on local and global information is also proposed. The
experimental studies carried out over two real forecasting
problems of the enrollments of University of Alabama observed
from 1971 to 1992 (EoU of Alabama) and killed in car road
accident in Belgium observed from 1974 to 2004 (CRA in
Belgium) show that the proposed forecasting method obtains
better forecasted results.

collected as a LTS which can be formally defined as follows.

V. CONCLUSIONS
The LTS-FMs are developed by utilizing the formalism of
directly computing with words based on hedge algebras.
However, the fuzziness parameter values of the existing LTSFMs are specified by human experts, so their forecasted
accuracies are not quite good. To increase forecasted accuracy,
this paper presents a method of optimizing the fuzziness
parameter values of LTS-FMs in order to improve their
forecasted results. Besides, a new method of computing the crisp
forecasted results is also proposed. The experimental results
over two forecasting problems, EoU of Alabama and CRA in
Belgium, show that our proposed LTS-FM outperforms their
counterparts. However, there are some characteristics of LTSFMs needed to be examined such as the number of used words,
the word semantic aspects, etc. They will be examined in our
next studies.
